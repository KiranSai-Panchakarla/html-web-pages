The Thirsty Crow

A Moral Story

Classic Fable

From Panchatantra Tales

Short Story
For Children

It was a hot summer day. A crow was feeling very thirsty.

He flew here and there in search of water, but could not find any.

The sun was blazing.
The crow became tired and sat under a tree.

Suddenly, he saw a pot in a garden. He flew to it and saw that the water was very low.

He got an idea. He picked up some pebbles and dropped them one by one into the pot.

But before that, he paused and thought: 

        "This water is too low.
         How can I drink it?
         If I drop pebbles,
         the water might rise...
         Yes! That's what I'll do!"

As he did this, the water level slowly rose up. Soon, he was able to drink the water.

He drank the water happily and flew away.

On his way, he told other birds about this trick.
They praised his cleverness and presence of mind.
The crow became famous in the forest for his smart thinking.

Moral: Where there is a will, there is a way.
